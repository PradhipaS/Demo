Dr. Muthulakshmi Reddy was a pioneering Indian medical professional, social reformer, and feminist who made significant contributions to women's rights, education, and healthcare in India. She was the first woman in India to graduate with a medical degree, the first woman legislator in British India, and the first woman to be appointed Vice-President of a legislative council. Her work included founding the Avvai Home orphanage and schools, and the Cancer Institute (WIA) in Chennai.
